F-Layout Pattern

Users typically scan content in an "F" shape

1. First horizontal scan

Most Important Content

This is where users' eyes land first. Place your primary headline and key information here.

2. Second horizontal scan

Secondary Information

Users scan horizontally again, but with less coverage than the first line.

3. Vertical scan

Additional Details

Users then scan vertically down the left side of the content, forming the stem of the "F".

This is why bullet points and subheadings work well in this area.

Sidebar Content

Less important information goes here. Users typically ignore this area during initial scanning.

  • Related links
  • Secondary navigation
  • Ads or promotions
